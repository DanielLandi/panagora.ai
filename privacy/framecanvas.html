Privacy Policy

FrameCanvas — Last updated: March 29, 2026

Overview

FrameCanvas is an iOS app that controls a Samsung The Frame over your local network. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is handled.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers
  • We do not use analytics, tracking, or advertising frameworks
  • We do not create user accounts or require registration
  • We have no backend servers that receive your data

Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your device only and never leaves it:

  • TV connection settings (IP address, MAC address) — stored in app preferences
  • OpenAI API key — stored securely in the iOS Keychain, encrypted at rest, never transmitted to any server other than OpenAI's API
  • Art groups and generated images — stored in the app's local documents directory
  • App preferences (theme, slideshow settings, parallelism) — stored in UserDefaults

Network Access

The app makes network connections in the following cases:

  • Local network (LAN): The app connects to your Samsung The Frame over your local Wi-Fi network using WebSocket (port 8002) to control art mode, upload images, and manage slideshows. This traffic stays within your home network.
  • OpenAI API (optional): If you provide an OpenAI API key, the app sends photos to OpenAI's image editing API (api.openai.com) to generate artistic transforms. Only the specific photos you select for transformation are sent. See OpenAI's privacy policy for how they handle image data.

Photos Library Access

The app requests access to your Photos library so you can browse and select photos for transformation or upload to your TV. Photos are read on-demand and are not copied or cached beyond what you explicitly save to an art group.
